About

A multifaceted artist dedicated to the future of live performing arts

Native New Yorker Benjamin T. Rossen is a pianist, composer, conductor, and fervent leader in live performing arts. He is on the teaching staff at Harvard University's Music Department and the former Executive Director of Lowell House Opera (LHO), the oldest performing opera company in New England.

Benjamin oversaw LHO's transformation through its largest multi-year fundraising campaign and four theatrical productions, including the 2024 world premiere of his original work The Unknowable: An Operatic Ballet in Two Acts. He was the music director and conductor of LHO's 2025 production of Postcard from Morocco by Dominick Argento.

He earned a Master of Music in Piano Performance from the New England Conservatory and a Bachelor of Arts in Economics and Music from Harvard University, graduating through the Harvard/NEC Dual Degree Program. He graduated from Harvard in three years.

Artistry

Four dimensions of musical expression and leadership

🎹

Pianist

Made his recital debut at age nine and concerto debut at Carnegie Hall at age ten. Has appeared as a guest artist with the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center, The Cliburn, and Music@Menlo. Quarterfinalist at the 2019 Cliburn International Junior Piano Competition.

♪

Composer

Creator of The Unknowable: An Operatic Ballet in Two Acts, which received its world premiere in 2024. His original overture for The Merry Widow won the 2023 Hugh F. MacColl Prize from the Harvard Music Department.

🎼

Conductor

Music director and conductor of Lowell House Opera's 2025 production of Postcard from Morocco. Founded and directed the Harvard Ballet Orchestra, and served as Music Director of Harvard College Opera.

✨

Artistic Leader

Former Executive Director of Lowell House Opera, the oldest performing opera company in New England. Led the company through its largest multi-year fundraising campaign and four theatrical productions. Currently on the teaching staff at Harvard University's Music Department.
